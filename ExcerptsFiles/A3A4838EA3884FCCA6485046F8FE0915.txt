Acute tubular necrosis 
- (“Coagulative” necrosis)
- [def] acute renal failure associated with potentially reversible injury to tubular epithelium.
- most common cause of acute renal failure.
- oliguria, rise in BUN, creatinine
- metabolic acidosis with Hyperkalemia.
- pct: lead gold heavy metal absorption, ORGANIC SOLVENTS
- loop of henle
- Pre renal acute renal failure (ischemic)
-  PRERENAL ARF: ratio bun/creatinine ratio up 30:1
- (Urine) dark brown cell casts and epithelial casts
-- ischemic, toxic
Ischemic congestive heart failure, hypotension, shock dehydration, renal artery stenosis
