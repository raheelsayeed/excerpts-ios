Adrenergic Transmission and drugs
- Tyrosine =={TyrosineHydroxylase}== DOPA
- DOPA =={Decarboxylase}== Dopamine (precurser of NE)
- Dopamine =={V-Dopamine-β-Hydroxylase} == NE


DIRECT ACTING (AGONISTS/BLOCKERS)
- α₁ or β₁

1. Methyl-P-Tyrosine
Not chemically used.
Blocks TyrosineHydroxylase
↓DOPA ↓NE release -- ↓ α/β stimulation

2. MAO inhibitors & Releasers.
Inhibits MAO Enzyme: ↑NE Pool
Releasers: ↑NE release activity
Exagerated Cardiovascular responses: Life threatening

3. Reupdate Blockers
↑ NE in synapse. ↑ Stimulation

5. α₂ Agonists & Antagonists
LOCATED PRESYNAPTICALLY not On Effector Cells
- AKA Autoreceptors
- Primary job: NEGATIVE FEEDBACK
- Control & Synthesis of NE release
- Blocking α₂ receptor: ↑NE Release & ↑α, β Stimulation

6. Reserpine

7. Guanethidine
