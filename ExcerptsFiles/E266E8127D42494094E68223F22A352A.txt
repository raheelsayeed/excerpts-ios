Carbonic Anhydrase inhibitors
In secondary active transport, Na is reabsorbed by excretion of H ion. 

Blocking CA inhibits creation of H+ and HCO3 in the cell.

Unavailability of H disables reabsorption of Na. 
- <H+ formation inside PCT cell
- bicarbonaturia results
- Bicarbonate is not reabsorbed, low pH
- Metabolic Acidosis

Uses:
Glaucoma
Acute mountain sickness
Metabolic alkalosis

Complications:
- metabolic acidosis
- hypokalemia
- renal stones
- hypercholremia


ACUTE MOUNTAIN SICKNESS
- >ALVEOLAR PO2 : hypoxemia, resp centers cause hyperventilation loosing PCO2.
- loss of co2 results in respiratory Alkalosis
- HYPOXIA IN LUNGS CAUSES VASOCONSTRICTION: INC HYDROSTATIC CAPILLARY PRESSURE: EDEMA

acetazolamide drains edema, treats Respiratory Alkalosis by compensating with Metabolic Acidosis

Hypercholremic Acidosis: classic in CA inhibitor drugs ’Metabolic Acidosis and HypoKalemia’
