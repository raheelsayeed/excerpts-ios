Hypertension Treatment Strategy
MAP = CO x TPR
          = (SV x HR) x TPR. 
TPR is proportional to Viscosity of blood and Length and inversely proportional to the 4th power of radius.

Goal: reduce Afterload and Preload.
Afterload = reduce TPR and CO to reduce Afterload.
Preload = reduce body fluid volume.

Consequences if BP is low:
1. Reflex tachycardia
2. Edema

Lowering BP via dec Sympathetic activity
1. No reflex tachycardia
2. Side effect : orthostatic hypertension
3. Increased parasympathetic activity (increased secretions, GI problems

Lowering BP using drugs without Sympathetic action
1. Reflex tachycardia
2. No orthostatic hypertension
3. No parasympathetic over activity related symptoms
