Angiotensin Receptor blockers
Blocks action of Angiotensin II by binding on AT-1 receptors
Same action as ACEI except no interference in bradykinin degradation. No symptoms of inflammation.
Dec aldosterone
Vasodilation (AT-1 receptors on BV cause vasoconstriction on stimulation)

Uses:
1. Mild to moderate HTN
2. Beneficial for patients with diabetic Nephropathy (ARBs and ACEI improve renal blood supply), dilation of Afferent arteriol.
3. Prevent remodeling in CHF

SE:
1. Hyperkalemia
2. Dry cough (bradykinin -> limitions)
3. Acute Renal failure
4. Angiogram

Renin
Angiotension II renin -> Angiotensin II constricts efferent arterioles ,   P-glomerular capillary increases, increased GFR filtration.
If renin is blocked then no sufficient filtration
