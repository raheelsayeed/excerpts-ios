Class III antiArrhythmic
Blocks voltage gated K+ channel blockers.
Effect:
- dec Ik slowing phase 3 (efflux of potassium)
- inc APD, inc ERP

Eg. Amiodarone: mimics I, II, III, IV.
Increased volume of distribution
Increased protein binding, tissue protein binding.

Major Disadvantage is t half of more than 80 days. Slow elimination.
Hapten activity: causes pulmonary fibrosis (like bleomycin) resulting in restrictive pulmonary disease.
Thyroid dysfunction
Hepatic necrosis
Corneal deposits

Smurf skin, blue pigmentation of skin.

Other class III Sotalol, k and beta blocker for life threatening ventricular Arrhythmias
