Metabolic Acidosis Potassium
Hyperkalemia
In distal Nephron, in a state of metabolic acidosis, more H+ ions are pumped into the tubule, and less of Ka is secreted out to the tubule resulting in K accumulation (Hyperkalemia).

Metabolic Acidosis has hyperkalemia due to exchange of excess of H+ for intracellular K

Metabolic Acidosis is Diuresis dependent, if Diuresis? K is secreted, and reduces Hyperkalemia.

METABOLIC ALKALOSIS PROMOTE HYPOKALEMIA
