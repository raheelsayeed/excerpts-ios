Angina
Angina is the principal syndrome of ischemic heart disease.

Types: 
1. Stable angina: due to atherosclerosis/ vessel occlusion sometimes with superimposed thrombosis. Most common sites are Left Anterior descending coronary artery (45%), right coronary artery (30%), circumflex artery (15%)
2. Variant Angina or Prinzmetal: reversible decreased coronary blood flow.

Treatment strategy:
1. Increase oxygen delivery by decreasing vasospasm (nitrates, CCB)
2. Decrease Oxygen requirement by decreasing workload, lower TPR and CO and use beta blocker for reflex tachycardia. (Nitrates, CCB, beta blocker)
